Neuroplasticity & Mindset

30 June 2024

Advances in neuroscience have shed light on two key concepts that play a significant role in learning: neuroplasticity and growth mindset. These concepts are particularly relevant in the dynamic field of software development.

This blog will explore their impact on learning, contrasting traditional university studies with the practical approach taken by institutions like Dev Academy.

Neuroplasticity:
The Brain's Remarkable Ability to Adapt

Neuroplasticity refers to the brain's ability to reorganise itself by forming new neural connections throughout life. Historically, the brain was thought to be relatively static after a certain age, but contemporary research has shown that our brains remain malleable well into adulthood. For aspiring developers, this is encouraging. Whether learning a new programming language or understanding complex algorithms, the brain's plasticity allows for continual improvement through practice and perseverance.

Growth Mindset:
The Foundation of Effective Learning

Coined by psychologist Carol Dweck, a growth mindset is the belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through dedication and hard work. This contrasts with a fixed mindset, where individuals believe their abilities are static.

A growth mindset fosters resilience, perseverance, and a love for learning—qualities essential in software development. When faced with challenges, those with a growth mindset see them as opportunities to grow rather than obstacles.

Traditional university settings provide a structured environment with set schedules and standardised testing. While this approach has its merits, it can sometimes limit opportunities for practical application.

In contrast, self-directed learning and programmes like Dev Academy encourage learners to take control of their education. This approach aligns well with neuroplasticity, as students engage with new materials and solve problems, reinforcing learning and promoting the formation of new neural connections.

The Developer's Learning Journey:
Practical Applications

At Dev Academy, we are immersed in coding from day one, working on real projects. This method is highly effective because it leverages both neuroplasticity and a growth mindset.

For example, when learning a new programming language, a student might initially struggle. However, through repeated practice and problem-solving, they begin to understand and internalise these new skills. The brain forms new neural pathways, making once challenging tasks more intuitive. This is very true for me personally… so far at least.

The iterative process of coding – writing code, testing it, debugging, and refining – embodies the growth mindset. Each error or bug is an opportunity to learn and improve.

Personal Refelctions

Understanding and embracing neuroplasticity and a growth mindset are essential for anyone learning to become a developer. The brain's ability to adapt and grow, coupled with a mindset that values effort and learning from mistakes, provides a powerful foundation for acquiring new skills and overcoming challenges.

Reflecting on my own journey, the difference between university studies and learning to code at Dev Academy is stark. At university, the focus was primarily on absorbing information and regurgitating it during exams, with little emphasis on practical application.

The curriculum at Dev Academy is a complete contrast and is designed to mimic the realities of a developer's job, with a strong emphasis on collaboration, practical projects, and continuous learning. This approach makes the learning process more engaging and effective
